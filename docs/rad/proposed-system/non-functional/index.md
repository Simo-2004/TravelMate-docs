# 3.3 Non-Functional Requirements

Non-functional requirements state the qualities the system must exhibit, as distinct from the functions it must perform. They constrain how well, how safely, and under what conditions those functions are delivered.

They are organised according to the **FURPS+** classification: usability, reliability, performance, and supportability are quality attributes of the system, while implementation, interface, packaging, and legal are pseudo-requirements — constraints imposed on the development and delivery of the system rather than properties of the system itself.

Each section states first the requirements satisfied by the delivered system, then — under **Deferred to future releases** — those belonging to the envisioned platform described in the Feasibility Study, which a later evolution of the system would have to meet. No requirement appears among the satisfied ones unless the delivered system meets it.

Requirements are expressed in terms of what the implementation must guarantee, not of the particular libraries or products that realise it; those choices are recorded in the System Design Document.

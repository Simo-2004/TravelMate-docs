# 1.3 Objectives & Success Criteria

## Objectives

1. Admit a Traveler through an account held on their own device, and protect the credentials that identify them.
2. Allow a Traveler to describe themselves, and to discover trips and companions matching what they are looking for.
3. Allow a Traveler to set aside the trips and companions they wish to revisit.
4. Allow a Traveler to converse with a companion about a possible journey.
5. Protect on the device the data the Traveler entrusts to the application.
6. Establish the persistence and protection foundations on which a networked release can later be built.
7. Present all of this through an interface that is direct and predictable on the supported devices.

## Success criteria

- A Traveler can register, sign out, and sign in again, recovering their profile unchanged.
- Profile, saved items, preferences, and conversations survive restarting the application.
- Credentials cannot be recovered from the stored data by anyone holding it.
- Personal data and conversation content are unreadable to anyone inspecting the stored data without the key held by the operating system.
- Data written by an earlier version of the application is carried forward without loss when the application is updated.
- Search returns results without perceptible delay and without any network connection.
- The interface remains usable across the range of supported screen sizes.

## Criteria for the envisioned platform

The following pertain to the platform once it acquires a server and a population of users, and are not assessed against the delivered system: matching a large majority of searches with compatible profiles, responding to searches within a stated time over the network, retaining users beyond their first month, and supporting many thousands of simultaneous users.

# 1.6 Overview

The remainder of this document is organised as follows.

**Chapter 2 — Current System** examines how travellers presently look for companions, the shortcomings of those approaches, and the opportunity the platform addresses.

**Chapter 3 — Proposed System** is the substance of the document. It opens with an overview of the proposed system and the scope of this lifecycle (3.1), states the functional requirements grouped by module (3.2) and the non-functional requirements grouped by quality (3.3), and formalises them in the analysis models (3.4): the scenarios and use cases that describe what the system does, the object model that describes what it is made of, the dynamic model that describes how it behaves over time, and the navigational structure of its interface.

**Chapter 4 — Glossary** defines the domain and technical terms used throughout.

Requirements are presented in two groups: those satisfied by the delivered system, and those collected under *Deferred to future releases* which belong to the envisioned platform. The analysis models in 3.4 describe the system at the level of the application domain; the mechanisms by which it is built are the subject of the System Design Document and the Object Design Document.
